The gotcha here is that the car is moving at speed and the driver needs to plan the timing of their commands just right so that there is no miscommunication between passengers.

For demonstration purposes lets say that at the end of every word is a road network intersection or (space) where a turn can be made and between each letter is an address or (character) where another turn can be made. Now, if we are moving at speed which of the following options is the most correct.
  1. A: turn right at the light
  2. B: at the light turn right
  3. C: in 100 feet turn right
  4. D: make a right turn
Obviously there is no one correct answer because it is a bit of a mix. 3 -> C is just a reminder to an upcoming event. However 1 -> A is wrong because it gets confusing for the driver to hear "turn right" first because they might think it is an emediate turn such as 4 -> D. So 2 -> B is right because it is best to give a location or (noun) first and then the direction or (verb adverb) second for correctness.
